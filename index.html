About

I am an adaptable and enthusiastic individual who loves solving problems and developing my skillset. I am in the process of transitioning from working as an Ecologist into a career in Tech, as a Front-End Software Developer. I have an undergraduate degree in Biology (BSc) and a postgraduate degree in Ecological Consultancy (MSc), both of which were obtained from Newcastle University.

My initial interest in programming was sparked at university, where although I worked on interesting projects, I didn't take the time to fully learn the fundamentals of programming and understand it on a deeper level. This initial spark of interest brought me back to programming after university, where I began learning the fundamentals of programming through Python3, before deciding that I wanted to pursue web development specifically, which I have learnt through a range of online resources, including the 'Front-End Engineer' career path course on CodeCademy, along with many youtube videos, articles and countless pieces of documentation!

I'm experienced with working as a team within a business environment and can communicate effectively with colleagues to ensure collaborative tasks are completed smoothly. I have experience with a range of front-end technologies, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript and React. I am also familiar with general team programming concepts such as agile workflows and version control using Git. I would love the opportunity to develop software in a team of likeminded people, contributing to solutions and learning from their experitise.
